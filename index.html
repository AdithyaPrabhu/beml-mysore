🌳 Save 137 Trees in Mysuru

Help raise your voice against the proposed felling of 137 mature trees in Koorgalli, Mysuru for the BEML Industrial Health Centre project.

What is happening?

Bharat Earth Movers Limited (BEML) has sought permission from the Forest Department to fell 137 trees for construction of an “Industrial Health Centre” within its premises at Koorgalli village in Yelwal Hobli of Mysuru Taluk.

According to the proposal, the trees include:
47 Teak Trees
13 Acacia Trees
10 Mahogany Trees
50 Wild Varieties
9 Peltophorum Trees
6 Silver Oak Trees
1 Orchid Tree
1 Honge Tree
⏰ Citizens can submit objections or suggestions before 5:30 PM on May 25

Why people are concerned

  • Mature trees provide cooling, groundwater recharge, oxygen generation, carbon absorption and biodiversity support that cannot be replaced for decades.
  • Mysuru is already witnessing increasing tree loss and rising urban temperatures.
  • The city already has multiple established hospitals and healthcare facilities accessible from the BEML–Koorgalli region.
  • Environmental activists have recently raised concerns over several other large-scale tree cutting proposals in Mysuru.
  • Once mature tree cover is destroyed, the ecological damage is effectively irreversible for the current generation.
